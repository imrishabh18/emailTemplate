IP audit: FTO

We have received the IP audit (a summary of the report available on request) report from Keltie LLP. It includes various aspects of perfexia’s technology in relation to patentability, as well as any potential freedom-to-operate (FTO) issues in view of IP held by a few specifically identified competitors operating in the field.

Patentability

Patentability: An aspect of perfeXia’s current activities that could be a potentially patentable candidate involves the incorporation of the photoplethysmogram (PPG) signal measurement and vital sign monitoring into a workflow / pipeline that can be provided to medical practitioners / organisations; and the integration of this monitoring and the results obtained with current secure storage methods for patient records. NB: The patentability of this aspect will also be dependent on what disclosures perfexia’s competitors in the field (both the two specifically identified companies, as well as any other entities operating in the field) have made regarding their own systems and methods.

Plans for growth

GP practices will be charged £100/GP/year to receive unlimited vital signs request from unlimited patients. This will help them grow their business and in turn, will increase our userbase (app downloads) and subsequently we will be able to offer premium services viz. prescription delivery etc. to gen Y & Z that are more prone to a “now culture” which expects online access to services around the clock.

Thinking ahead

Currently, users will not be receiving clinical decisions or diagnostic services through DocMe. Therefore, the legal opinion is that Class I (self certification) MHRA declaration and CE mark will be sufficient for us to go to market. After the clinical study in March 2021, DocMe will need to be re-classified as Class IIa medical device which can be used as a diagnostic tool.
