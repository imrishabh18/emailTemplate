Advancement in Techology


Our progress in Techology


App, pre-clinical study: improving accuracy


We recently launched DocMe app (iOS) through TestFlight. We have no immediate plan for an Android launch. DocMe (beta) contains two live modules (heart rate and respiratory rate) approved by Apple AppStore. Other modules are either in the final stage of production (e.g. SpO2) or in the process of gaining approval from Apple (e.g. emotional-stress analysis). Our next step is to calibrate existing modules as well as modules in the R&D against approved medical devices for the same purpose.
Simultaneously, we will be running a pre-clinical study amongst 30 healthy human subjects we recruited from different colleges at the University of Cambridge. The clinical study protocol has been carefully designed and approved by a professional healthcare regulatory person from our advisory board. Results from this pre-clinical study will be used for optimising our proprietary algorithm to determine key vital signs. To facilitate this scientific and technological work, we have formed a partnership with the Technical University in Munich where we are supporting one of our employees during his master's project, which focuses on this very work.
