Advancement
in Technology

Our progress in Technology

App, pre-clinical study:
improving accuracy

We recently launched DocMe app (iOS) through TestFlight. We have no immediate plans for an Android launch. DocMe 2.0 (beta) contains two live modules (heart rate and respiratory rate) approved by Apple AppStore. Other modules are either in the final stage of production (e.g. SpO2) or in the process of gaining approval from Apple (e.g. emotional-stress analysis). Our next step is to calibrate existing modules as well as modules in the R&D against approved medical devices for the same purpose.

For this, we will be running a pre-clinical study with 30 healthy human subjects we recruited from different colleges at the University of Cambridge. One of our advisory board members specialised in designing clinical studies carefully designed the clinical study protocol. Results from this pre-clinical study will be used for optimising our proprietary algorithm to determine key vital signs. To facilitate this scientific and technological work, we have formed a partnership with the Technical University in Munich where we are supporting one of our employees during his master's project, which focuses on this very work.

Capital

Our progress regarding cash and other financial assets

Towards revenue generation

We are still in the pre-revenue stage with a burn rate of £7,500(Oct'21). Based on our current roadmap, we expect to become a revenue-generating company by March 2021, with other items remaining broadly in line with our planning.

To become a revenue-generating company by March 2021, we will need to clear our pre-clinical study, clinical trial with Oxford University Hospitals and secure regulatory approval. For this, we will need a capital investment of approximately £250,000 by Feb 2021. We plan to secure this capital requirement by prize money, grants and additional investments. *

Capital requirement

We are in the process of applying for a Go-Create grant (deadline 6th Nov) through ISfB, Oxford as we are taking part in Oxford-based innovation hub programmes viz. Panacea Star Develop programme. This grant usually has a cap of £50,000 with a minimum 3:1 co-investment requirement by the applicant business. Therefore, we will have to demonstrate to raise around £100,000 if we want to maximise this grant opportunity.

However, if we secure a SmartGrant from InnovateUK (deadline 25th Nov), the capital requirement from external investment gets reduced dramatically. Panacea Star prize, if won, will effectively nullify any extra top-up investment altogether. However, to carry out our pre-clinical study - which will increase our chances to win the first prize at Panacea Star - we will require a top-up investment of £20,000. Terms for this top-up investment will be the same as the F&F round.*
