Investor Update:
February 2021

Today we are updating you on PERFEXIA’s journey through the first two months of the Q1 of 2021. In the last two months, we have been able to launch DocMe, carry out a data training session despite the lockdown and based on that we have been able to improve the accuracy of DocMe which we aim to reach medical-grade accuracy by the end of this month. Given the progress made in January and February, we now update our guidance for the current quarter as below:


• Getting our first paid pilot customer
• Filing paperwork for the FDA approval (pre-cert)
• Submitting our application for the YCombinator programme

Advancement
in Technology

Our progress in Technology

App, pre-clinical study:
improving accuracy

DocMe (2.1.4) now has heart rate (HR), respiratory rate (RR), oxygen saturation levels (SpO2), blood pressure (BP) with near medical-grade accuracy. We are compiling a report on these measurements and like before, we will be updating you on that as soon as we receive clearance from our IP lawyers. This was achieved in line with our product roadmap and timeframe set out in the last update. More details are available on request.

We have now received clearance from the Research Ethics Committee (REC) for the clinical trial at OUH. This study will validate and compare the accuracy of DocMe with previously validated medical devices used for measurements of HR, RR, SpO2 and BP in healthy volunteers. This will give us a competitive advantage as the first mover to medically validate all the important vital signs measured using one simple and attractive consumer app.
On the product development side, we now have implemented the ORCHA protocol for better rating and trustworthiness amongst health apps. Please download, try the new UI if you have not done so yet and if you love our product, please don't forget to rate our app on the App Store.

App development

Additionally, I'm pleased to announce that we have an internal Android version that is being stress tested and will be launched on Google Play Store in early April. This was achieved in record time ahead of our original product roadmap due to popular demands.
Finally, we now have a timeframe to deliver actionable insights (0.1) based on vital signs and Apple Health integration. We are still in the development phase for this and expecting to finish this work by the end of May - our expected timeline.

Business

Our progress in business for growth
projection and related aspects

Strategic partnership

For B2C, our strategic partnership with King's College Cambridge resulted in 15% week on week growth in downloads. This exceeded our anticipated 10 WoW growth rate. However, this has since been plateaued. To address this, we have run user interviews (n=122) to understand the user pain points better. Our studies show that students are 78% more likely to download DocMe if it provides stress management capabilities. This is within our product roadmap - actionable insights (0.1). Simultaneously, we are reaching out to other colleges and societies to increase the number of downloads and expand our consumer base. Please introduce DocMe to anyone interested in remote vital signs monitoring.

On the B2Bend, we have been able to engage with three HealthTech companies. We are in the different stages of discussions with Nye.Health, Your.MD and Push Doctors - companies that showed great interest in DocMe. We are in the advanced stage discussion with iPlato that provide 6.2m online GP/Doctor consultations for the NHS annually. Last year, even with COVID, they saw a drop in these. The feedback was that the GP’s didn’t have all the tools they needed to provide online consultations. They recognised DocMe as the right solution for their problem, particularly when we are to receive clinical validation for blood oxygen levels and blood pressure as well. Please refer/recommend us to any remote GP service you may be aware of.

IP protection

On the IP front, we are in the process of finalising two patent application that involves the incorporation of the photoplethysmogram (PPG) signal measurement and vital sign monitoring into a workflow/pipeline that can be provided to medical practitioners/organisations; and the integration of this monitoring and the results obtained with current secure storage methods for patient records. We will be filing those patents in the UK, US and EU primarily, later in Japan, Canada and AUS.

Capital

Our progress regarding cash and other financial assets

As per our guidance from December 2020, we remain convinced that we can become a revenue-generating company by the end of this quarter. We estimate B2B to be our main source of revenue at this stage. This will, however, be complemented and later overtaken by a direct to consumer approach.
